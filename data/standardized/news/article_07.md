The meeting also marked progress in the development of the Be Milk dairy manufacturing plant at Prodezi Industrial Park.

The session took place at the Tay Ninh Provincial People's Committee headquarters, with participation from the Economic Zone Authority and representatives of related departments. The international delegation included Swiss Asia Partner SA, diplomatic representatives, the French Chamber of Commerce and Industry, and enterprises involved in the project's technology and value chain.

Representatives of Prodezi and Swiss Asia Partner SA signed the Be Milk project agreement, witnessed by Tay Ninh provincial leaders and international diplomatic and investment delegations. Photo courtesy of Prodezi

Participants discussed the province's investment environment, industrial development orientation, and the implementation roadmap for the Be Milk project, in which Swiss Asia Partner SA will act as investor and market developer.

The project is structured through a partnership and franchise agreement with Sodiaal Group (France), owner of the Candia brand, incorporating European production and quality standards. A land reservation agreement between Swiss Asia Partner SA and Prodezi was also signed during the event.

The meeting comes as Tay Ninh continues to attract investment in Southern Vietnam. In 2025, the province recorded total newly registered and adjusted foreign direct investment (FDI) of approximately US$1.7 billion.

Local authorities have focused on expanding industrial land, improving connectivity, and prioritizing high value-added manufacturing sectors, alongside projects aligned with environmental, social and governance (ESG) standards.

Philippe Phan Van Ho, CEO of Swiss Asia Partner SA, affirmed the long-term partnership with Prodezi to implement an international-standard dairy manufacturing model in the locality. Photo courtesy of Prodezi

Vietnam's dairy sector is projected to grow steadily, with IMARC Group estimating the market will reach $13.37 billion by 2033, driven by rising domestic demand. This trend presents opportunities for new processing projects with advanced technologies and international standards.

The Be Milk plant is expected to require total investment of about $100 million and will be developed in two phases. The project plans to apply European-standard technologies, supported by international partners including Sodiaal Group (France), IPEM Group (France), Tetra Pak (Sweden) and Takenaka Corporation (Japan).

Philippe Phan Van Ho, CEO of Swiss Asia Partner SA, said the selection of Tay Ninh was based on both market potential and the industrial infrastructure's ability to support long-term operational requirements.

The project is designed to integrate seamlessly into the infrastructure of Prodezi Industrial Park. Specifically, wastewater systems are designed to meet stringent environmental standards prior to connection to centralized treatment facilities, while energy efficiency and resource optimization solutions are embedded to support sustainable performance.

Futhermore, the park's industrial symbiosis model also allows for integration with related manufacturing activities, fostering a circular and sustainable production ecosystem.

Prodezi's eco-oriented industrial infrastructure serves as a foundation for attracting high-quality FDI inflows. Photo courtesy of Prodezi

Truong Khac Nguyen Minh, Deputy General Director of Prodezi Long An, said the company has developed its industrial park model based on ESG-oriented infrastructure and integrated production systems, which are compatible with the requirements of international investors.

Once operational, the Be Milk plant is expected to contribute to Vietnam's dairy processing capacity through the application of international technologies and management practices, while also supporting value chain development from raw materials to distribution.

At the provincial level, the project is expected to create jobs, contribute to local revenues, and support Tay Ninh's strategy of attracting higher-quality investment and developing sustainable industry.
